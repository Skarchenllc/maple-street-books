Our Story

Independent. Community-rooted. Proudly bookish since 2008.

How Maple Street Books Began

Maple Street Books opened its doors in the spring of 2008, born from a simple belief: every neighborhood deserves a place where stories live and readers gather. Founders Claire and Daniel Hartwell converted a century-old hardware store into the warm, shelf-lined refuge you visit today — exposed brick, creaking floors, and all.

What started as a modest collection of literary fiction and local history has grown into a carefully curated inventory spanning every genre, age group, and interest. We stock more than 12,000 titles at any given time, with a dedicated section for regional authors and a rotating display of staff obsessions.

Through two recessions, a pandemic, and the relentless rise of online retail, Maple Street Books has endured — and thrived — because of you. Our customers are our neighbors, our friends, and our reason for showing up every morning.

🌿 Deep in the Community

We believe a bookstore is more than a retail space — it's a civic institution. Over the years we've partnered with dozens of local organisations to put books in the hands of people who need them most:

  • Maple Street Reads: Annual used-book drive donating 2,000+ books to local schools each autumn.
  • Story Corner: Free Saturday-morning storytime for children under 8, running every week since 2010.
  • First Chapter Fund: Subsidised books for families referred by the city's social services department.
  • Author in Residence: Month-long programme hosting a local writer with free public workshops.
  • Book Club Network: We sponsor and supply 14 independent neighbourhood book clubs.
